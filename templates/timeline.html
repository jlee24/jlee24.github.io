May 2018 - Pigott Theater, Stanford

I feel very lucky to have been part of the cast of " The World of Extreme Happiness," written by Frances Ya-Chu Cowhig and directed by Vineet Gupta. Without revealing too much, it's a story about a migrant worker named Sunny who leaves rural China to work in an urban factory and the transformation she undergoes in pursuit of her vision of success. I'm playing Sunny!

May 2018 - Stanford

Published a piece of prose in STATIC's spring zine, themed "Origin." "So often, our relationships with our origins are ascribed to us by outside powers who wish to simplify complex concepts such as home, culture, ancestry, language, and ethnicity in ways that ignore our different experiences of them. In this zine, we showcase essays, stories, artwork, and poetry which narrate origins in ways that aren't structured by normative ideas of what it means to be or to become. Here you'll find elegies for homelands and the dances of gods; indictments of colonialism and ink drawn from rivers."

November 2017 - The Nitery, Stanford

I was the producer for the Asian American Theatre Project's fall show, The Chronicles of Kalki, "a comic-book infused girl-gang thriller that follows the story of Kalki, the new girl at school who may or may not be the final avatar of Vishnu, come to rid the world of demons and evil. The Chronicles of Kalki tells a tale of female independence, identity, and rebellious self-assertion, taking on the universal question of just how exactly people survive high school." Big thanks to Stanford ARTS and the Nitery Experimental Theater (NeXT).
